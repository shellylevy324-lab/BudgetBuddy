# Training Station Platform Design Language

## Purpose

The Training Station Platform supports adolescents and young adults as they build skills for work, community participation, daily living, communication, and greater independence.

Every design decision should help the trainee feel like a capable young adult preparing for the future.

---

## Platform Language

### Preferred Terms

| Avoid | Prefer |
|---|---|
| Teacher | Job Coach |
| Student | Trainee |
| Student Home | Training Station |
| Dashboard | Work Hub |
| Module | Training Area |
| Lesson | Training Activity |
| Assignment | Today's Training |
| Start Session | Begin Training |
| End Session | Complete Training |
| Correct | Completed |
| Incorrect | Try Again |
| Reward | Motivation |
| Good Job! | Nice Work! |
| Progress Data | Training Progress |

Administrative areas may continue to use the word "student" when needed for school records, reporting, or database organization.

---

## Platform Roles

### Trainee

The young adult completing training activities.

### Job Coach

The teacher, paraprofessional, therapist, or other adult supporting the trainee.

### Program Coordinator

A staff member responsible for managing trainees, job coaches, settings, reports, or school-level information.

### Family

A parent, guardian, or caregiver who may receive progress information.

---

## Core Values

### 1. Dignity First

The platform must respect trainees as adolescents and young adults.

Avoid childish graphics, language, celebrations, and sound effects.

### 2. Independence First

The platform should reduce unnecessary adult support and encourage trainees to complete tasks as independently as possible.

### 3. Authentic Training

Activities should resemble tasks trainees may encounter in work, home, and community settings.

### 4. Accessibility

Screens must work with touch, mouse, keyboard, tablets, laptops, and desktop computers.

### 5. Calm Design

The interface should be simple, predictable, and free from unnecessary visual distractions.

### 6. Job Coach Partnership

The platform supports the relationship between the trainee and job coach. It does not replace human instruction, observation, or professional judgment.

### 7. Data With Purpose

Training data should help job coaches make instructional decisions and improve trainee independence.

---

## Visual Identity

### Overall Feel

The platform should feel:

- professional
- calm
- modern
- respectful
- age-appropriate
- welcoming
- easy to understand

It should not feel:

- childish
- overly clinical
- crowded
- cartoon-like
- game-heavy
- visually overwhelming

- headers
- primary buttons

- important labels

`#234E70`

### Secondary Color

Teal

Used for:

- progress indicators
- secondary buttons
- supportive highlights

Suggested value:

`#2A7F8E`

### Accent Color

Warm gold

Used sparingly for:

- important attention areas
- selected training activities
- completion indicators

`#D9A441`

Soft gray

Suggested value:

`#F4F6F8`

### Card Background

White

Suggested value:

`#FFFFFF`

### Main Text

Dark gray

Suggested value:

`#263238`

### Secondary Text

Medium gray

Suggested value:

`#5F6B73`

### Success

Suggested value:

`#3A7D44`

`#B36B00`

---

## Typography

Use system fonts so the platform works consistently across Windows, Mac, tablets, and Chromebooks.

Preferred font stack:

`Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif`

### Heading Sizes

- Main page title: 36px
- Section title: 26px
- Card title: 22px
- Standard text: 18px
- Small supporting text: 15px

Text should remain readable without zooming.

Avoid decorative fonts.

---

## Layout

### Maximum Page Width

`1100px`

### Standard Page Padding

- Desktop: 32px
- Tablet: 24px
- Mobile: 16px

### Standard Spacing

Use multiples of 8 pixels:

- 8px
- 16px
- 24px
- 32px
- 48px

### Cards

Cards should have:

- white background
- rounded corners
- clear headings
- generous spacing
- subtle shadow
- no unnecessary decoration

---

## Buttons

### Primary Buttons

Used for the most important action on a screen.

Examples:

- Begin Training
- Continue
- Complete Training
- Save Plan

Primary buttons should:

- be large
- use clear action language
- have strong contrast
- work well on touchscreens
- have at least 48px height

### Secondary Buttons

Used for actions such as:

- Back
- Review
- Change Trainee
- View Progress

### Button Language

Buttons should begin with an action word.

Prefer:

- Begin Training
- Choose Activity
- Continue
- Save Changes
- Return to Training Station

Avoid:

- Click Here
- Submit
- Go
- Next Step

---

## Icons

Icons should support understanding, not replace text.

Use simple, familiar icons for:

- money
- shopping
- transportation
- communication
- daily living
- employment
- reading
- health
- community participation

Every icon should be paired with a written label.

Avoid cartoon characters and decorative emoji as permanent interface elements.

## Trainee Screen Rules

Trainee screens should:

- show one main task at a time
- use minimal text
- avoid scrolling whenever possible
- include large buttons
- maintain consistent button locations
- clearly identify the trainee
- clearly identify the job coach
- reduce unnecessary navigation
- provide predictable feedback
- avoid visible staff-only data

---

## Job Coach Screen Rules

Job Coach screens may include:

- trainee selection
- training plans
- prompt settings
- processing time
- reinforcement preferences
- progress summaries
- session history
- data export
- training-area settings

Job Coach screens should still remain simple and organized.

---

## Feedback Language

### Success

Preferred messages:

- Nice Work
- Training Complete
- Activity Complete
- You Finished Today's Training
- Ready for the Next Activity

### Error Correction

Preferred messages:

- Try Again
- Take Another Look
- Check the Price
- Ask Your Job Coach for Support

Avoid:

- Wrong
- Incorrect
- Failed
- Bad Choice

---

## Accessibility Requirements

The platform should:

- support touch and mouse input
- support keyboard navigation
- use visible focus indicators
- maintain strong color contrast
- avoid flashing animations
- allow extended processing time
- prevent accidental rapid-click responses when configured
- use readable language
- avoid relying only on sound
- avoid relying only on color
- work across common modern browsers

---

## Responsive Design

The platform should support:

- Windows laptops
- Mac computers
- Chromebooks
- iPads
- Android tablets
- large classroom displays

Cards should stack vertically on smaller screens.

Buttons should remain large enough for touch input.

---

## Standard Trainee Workflow

1. Select trainee
2. Open Training Station
3. Review Today's Training
4. Confirm Job Coach
5. Begin Training
6. Complete Training Activities
7. Review completion
8. Return to Training Station

---

## Standard Job Coach Workflow

1. Sign in
2. Select trainee
3. Review trainee profile
4. Choose training activities
5. Configure supports
6. Begin training
7. Monitor performance
8. Review data
9. Save or export progress

---

## Guiding Question

Before approving any screen, ask:

> Does this screen help the trainee feel like a capable young adult building skills for work, community participation, and greater independence?
