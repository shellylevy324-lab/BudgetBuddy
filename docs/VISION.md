# Budget Buddy Vision and Roadmap

## Purpose
Budget Buddy is an accessible, ABA-informed transition-skills platform for teaching functional community independence.

## Design Principles
- One-screen student activities whenever possible
- Low visual clutter and predictable layouts
- Large touch targets
- Student data stays on teacher-facing screens
- Assessment and teaching procedures remain distinct
- Prompts guide attention before guiding the response
- Student-specific settings should carry across future modules

## Current Instructional Level
### Level 1 — Single-Item Affordability
The student compares one item price with one available budget and answers Yes or No.

## Grocery Skill Progression
### Level 2 — Multiple Purchases
- Buy more than one item
- Subtract purchases from a running budget
- Display remaining money

### Level 3 — Grocery Shopping List
- Follow a teacher-created or randomized list
- Locate and check off multiple items
- Track task completion and sequence

### Level 4 — Checkout
- Choose cash or card
- Practice handing over cash
- Practice tap, insert, or swipe card routines
- Wait for approval
- Collect receipt and belongings

### Level 5 — Teacher-Controlled Budget Difficulty
Student profile options should include:
- Minimum budget
- Maximum budget
- Fixed or randomized budget
- Whole-dollar or decimal amounts
- Difficulty progression

### Level 6 — Shopping Decisions
- Choose between multiple items
- Identify affordable options
- Prioritize necessary items
- Make decisions with limited money

### Level 7 — Real-World Shopping Mode
- Carry an iPad or phone in a real store
- Follow a live shopping list
- Track purchases and remaining budget
- Complete checkout
- Signal task completion

## Instructional Modes
### Baseline
- No prompts
- No trial-by-trial reinforcement
- No error correction
- Quiet data collection
- Universal completion signal remains available

### Least-to-Most Teaching
1. Initial spoken SD
2. Visual price/budget comparison band
3. Secondary spoken prompt
4. Modeled response cue
5. Student-specific reinforcement after correct responses

## Reinforcement Roadmap
- Personalized written praise
- Optional sound
- Completion animation for all modes
- Future teacher voice recordings
- Future GIF or short video reinforcement
- Student-specific schedules and preferences

## Current Development Step
### v0.9 — Budget Difficulty Foundation
Student profiles now control:
- Fixed versus randomized budgets
- Minimum budget
- Maximum budget
- Whole-dollar versus 50-cent increments

This prepares the application for multiple-item purchasing and running remaining budgets.

## Current Development Step
### v0.10.1 — Level 2 Grocery List Affordability
- Level 1: Can one item fit within the budget?
- Level 2: Can every item on a short grocery list fit within the budget?

Future levels:
- Select which combination of items fits
- Add items to a cart
- Track a running remaining budget
- Complete checkout with cash or card


## Current Development Step
### v0.12 — Decision-Making Cart
Students now choose a required number of items while remaining within budget.

Next planned milestone:
- Checkout and purchasing routines
- Cash versus card
- Receipt and task completion


## Current Development Step
### v0.13 — Prompt Fading + Token Acquisition
Students may begin at a teacher-selected prompt level and fade toward independence. Tokens are contingent on correct first responses at the programmed level. Errors receive model correction without token delivery.

Future reinforcement options may include a school-approved media library, preview controls, volume settings, and reduced-motion alternatives.
