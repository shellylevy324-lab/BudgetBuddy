# Budget Buddy Instructional Framework

## Core Principles
1. Keep student activities on one screen whenever possible.
2. Minimize visual clutter and unnecessary movement.
3. Keep teacher data off student-facing screens.
4. Separate assessment procedures from teaching procedures.
5. Guide attention before guiding the response.
6. Individualize prompts and reinforcement.
7. Preserve predictable layouts across activities.
8. Build toward functional community independence.

## Baseline
Baseline is used to measure independent performance.

During baseline:
- No prompts
- No trial-by-trial praise
- No sounds tied to correct responses
- No error correction
- Quiet data collection
- Universal completion signal remains available

## Least-to-Most Teaching
1. Initial spoken SD: “Can I afford this item?”
2. Visual comparison of item price and available budget
3. Secondary spoken prompt: “Look at your budget. Do you have enough to buy this item?”
4. Modeled response cue
5. Student-specific reinforcement after a correct response

## Reinforcement
Reinforcement should be individualized and should support engagement without overwhelming the student.

Current options:
- Written praise
- Written praise plus gentle chime
- None

Future options:
- Teacher-recorded praise
- Short GIF or animation
- Brief preferred video
- Individualized schedules

## Completion Signal
The completion animation is a universal signal that the activity is finished. It is not contingent on accuracy and may be used in both Baseline and Teaching.
